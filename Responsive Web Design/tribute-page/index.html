Alan Mathison Turing

The father of computer science

Here's a timeline of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1927 - Becomes deeply interested in the work of Albert Einstein.
  • 1934 - Graduates with distinction in Mathematics from King's College, Cambridge.
  • 1938 - Goes to Princeto University in America to study mathematic and is awarded a PhD.
  • 1939 - Joins the Government Codes and Ciphers Schools.
  • 1940 - Wiht Gordon Welchman, develops the Bombe to dechipher the messages sent by the Germans using their Enigma machine.
  • 1945 - Is awarded the OBE for his wartime services
  • 1946 - Publishes a paper with the first detailed design of a stored program computer.
  • 1949 - Is made deputy director of the Computing Laboratory at Manchester University
  • 1950 - Develops the Turing Test for determining whether or not a computer is capable of thinking like a human.
  • 1952 - Alan is arrested for gross indecency. He loses his security clearance so he cannot work. He is offered chemical treatment instead of going to prison. The treatment makes him very unwell.
  • 1954 - Alan dies in his home in Wilmslow, Cheshire, from poisoning. A half-eaten apple was found next to him laced with cyanide.
